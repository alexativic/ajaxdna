Our Technological Journey

Key milestones in our development of breakthrough biotechnological solutions

Automatic Pipelines for Bioinformatics

We saw that the problem lay in the difficulty to make biological engineering easy and accessible for everyone. So we tested whether there was a market fit for products improving these difficulties. We started by making interfaces for easy use of state-of-the-art protocols and bioinformatic tools.

Protein Engineering

We are automating and creating new pipelines for protein engineering, enabling precise modifications and optimization of biological molecules for enhanced functionality.

Metabolic Modeling

We have automated metabolic modeling creation and simulations, allowing for boundary exploration of what is and is not possible to do in cell systems, providing detailed insights into cellular processes and metabolic pathways.

Cell Parametrization

Sophisticated protein parametrization pipelines to fine-tune and detect all possible interactions driving cell functionality, allowing for precise characterization and optimization of cellular behavior.

→ We are here

First Digital Twin Creation

We realized that all the previous work was necessary to create virtual and complete simulations of cell behavior. This is a breakthrough achievement in creating the first comprehensive digital twin of biological systems, enabling unprecedented simulation and prediction capabilities. Learning not just what is possible, but what is expected from a living organism after an engineering process is performed.

LabOnACell Completion

The culmination of our technological journey and the definitive solution for engineering biological processes, providing our full value proposition. The completion of LabOnACell comes to a validated market, to a validated pain, and to solve the difficulty of handling biological nanotechnology.

Data Sources & Methodology

Market Data Sources

  • Textile Industry: McKinsey Global Institute, Textile World Magazine
  • Food Industry: FAO, USDA, Food Navigator
  • Meat Industry: Good Food Institute, McKinsey Alternative Proteins Report
  • Catalysts Industry: Chemical & Engineering News, IHS Markit
  • Construction Industry: Construction Dive, Green Building Council

Methodology

Data Processing: For each market, we collect data from 3 different sources, calculate the average market size and average CAGR. Market penetration is calculated as: (Bio Market Size / Traditional Market Size) × 100%. Once a market reaches 100% penetration, the bio market grows at the traditional market CAGR rate to maintain 100% penetration. Overall penetration is calculated as a market-size-weighted average of individual market penetrations.
